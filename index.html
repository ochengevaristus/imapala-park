About Impala Park

Located on the shores of Lake Victoria in the vibrant city of Kisumu, Kenya, Impala Park is a sanctuary for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. With over 40 hectares of scenic landscapes, the park offers a haven for a variety of wildlife, including impalas, zebras, giraffes, and over 100 species of birds.

The park also boasts a unique combination of open savannah grasslands, acacia woodlands, and wetlands, making it an ideal destination for both casual visitors and serious wildlife photographers. It's perfect for family picnics, school field trips, and anyone looking to reconnect with nature.

Park Highlights

  • Wildlife: Apart from impalas, visitors can spot zebras, giraffes, buffaloes, and even leopards. The park is also home to several primates such as vervet monkeys and baboons.
  • Bird Watching: With its proximity to Lake Victoria, Impala Park is a bird watcher's paradise. You can spot kingfishers, herons, egrets, and flamingos during their migratory periods.
  • Boat Rides: Take a serene boat ride on Lake Victoria to enjoy a stunning sunset or spot hippos along the shoreline.
  • Nature Trails: Explore walking trails that weave through the park, providing picturesque views of both the wildlife and the surrounding landscape.
  • Education & Conservation: The park is involved in conservation efforts, educating visitors on Kenya's rich biodiversity and how they can help protect it.

Plan Your Visit

Opening Hours: The park is open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Entry Fees:

  • Adults: 500 KES (Residents), 1,000 KES (Non-Residents)
  • Children: 250 KES (Residents), 500 KES (Non-Residents)

Location: Impala Park is located 2 kilometers from Kisumu City Centre, along the shores of Lake Victoria. It is easily accessible by road, and there is ample parking for visitors.

Guided Tours: The park offers guided tours by knowledgeable local rangers who provide insights into the animals, plant life, and conservation efforts at the park.
